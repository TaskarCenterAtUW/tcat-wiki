## Clark County Walk/Roll Event — Event Summary Report

### Overview

On January 20th, 2026, the Nondrivers Alliance (NDA) and the Taskar Center for Accessible Technology (TCAT) hosted **Clark County Walk/Roll Event**, an in-person event focused on improving pedestrian infrastructure data in Vancouver, WA for accessibility-focused routing in **Vancouver, WA**.

| Detail       | Info                       |
|--------------|----------------------------|
| **Date**     | January 20th, 2026         |
| **Format**   | in-person                  |
| **Location** | Gaiser Hall, Vancouver, WA |

### Walkabout

Participants walked a route through Vancouver, WA using **AVIV ScoutRoute** to collect on-the-ground pedestrian infrastructure observations, submitted to the **NDA Vancouver Walk/Roll** workspace.

#### Route

Gaiser Hall to Washington Street

- **Distance**: 1.6 miles

#### What Was Observed

Participants used AVIV ScoutRoute to submit observations about pedestrian infrastructure conditions along the route, including:

- Sidewalk surface condition and width
- Missing or damaged curb ramps
- Obstructions (poles, signs, vegetation)
- Tactile paving presence/absence
- Crosswalk visibility and signal accessibility

#### Walkabout Resources

- [AVIV ScoutRoute Guide](aviv-scoutroute.md) — app installation and usage

### Impact

The data collected during this event directly supports:

- **Accessible routing** — improved trip planning for wheelchair users, people in the blind/low-vision community, and others with mobility needs through [AccessMap](https://accessmap.app/)
- **Pedestrian navigation** — more complete sidewalk and crossing data enables safer, more accurate walking and rolling directions
- **Open data** — a richer, freely available dataset for Vancouver, WA and the surrounding region
