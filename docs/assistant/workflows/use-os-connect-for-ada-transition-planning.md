# Use OS-CONNECT for ADA transition planning (workflow)

## Short Answer

Use OS-CONNECT snapshots and QA/QC views to understand modeled pedestrian network coverage and prioritize data improvements that inform transition planning conversations. Pair every analytic step with professional ADA assessment where required.

## Significance

Agencies want defensible prioritization. A workflow that separates data insight from legal sign-off keeps both honest.

## What This Means

1. Establish geography and reporting period with your OS-CONNECT contacts.
2. Review completeness and component metrics in the QA/QC documentation for that release.
3. Identify gaps that affect program access routes or high-demand facilities.
4. Feed prioritized field verification and capital projects; document assumptions.

## What This Does Not Mean

- Not a checklist that replaces Title II program access review.
- Not a single software click that outputs a compliant plan.

## How To Use This

Assign a GIS lead for metrics and a program lead for facility realities. Revisit when the dataset version changes.

## Example

A city notes low modeled connectivity near major civic buildings, schedules field audits for those corridors, and updates internal transition plan narratives accordingly—without claiming OS-CONNECT “certified” compliance.
