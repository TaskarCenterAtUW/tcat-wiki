# Walksheds (concept)

## Short Answer

A walkshed is the area reachable from an origin along the pedestrian network within a budget — time, distance, or a custom cost — under chosen preferences. It is an analytical footprint, not a regulatory zone by itself.

## Significance

Walksheds support prioritization, equity conversations, and Safe Routes to School–style planning when paired with local policy. Mislabeling them as approvals or safety certifications undermines trust.

## What This Means

Results depend on graph data, cost functions, and scenario settings. Comparing walksheds is only meaningful when those inputs are aligned.

## What This Does Not Mean

- Not a school attendance boundary unless a jurisdiction explicitly defines it as such.
- Not a crash risk model.

## How To Use This

Use walksheds to explore "who or what is within reach" for scenarios, then ground decisions in local engineering, policy, and engagement.

## Example

Two origins five minutes apart on foot might have very different walksheds if one side lacks curb ramps in the modeled network for a given profile.

## Assistant Guidance

Always mention dependency on data and settings. For school topics, pair with the Safe Routes workflow page and abstain from binding district policy statements.
