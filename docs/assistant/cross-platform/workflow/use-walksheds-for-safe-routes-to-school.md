# Use Walksheds for Safe Routes to School

## Short Answer

Model school origins and student-weighted scenarios in Walksheds to visualize accessibility-aware reachability, compare infrastructure alternatives, and export maps for engagement. Follow local SRTS policy for decisions.

## Significance

Walksheds makes accessibility-aware reachability legible to non-GIS partners when framed responsibly.

## What This Means

1. Confirm dataset vintage and graph sources with stakeholders.
2. Configure profiles and costs aligned to your program's equity goals.
3. Run baseline and alternative scenarios for the same origins.
4. Pair maps with qualitative crossing stress knowledge and community workshops.

## What This Does Not Mean

- Not a substitute for student travel counts where required.
- Not an automatic ranking of which families should walk.

## How To Use This

Use outputs as conversation starters with families, principals, and public works — not silent adjudication.

## Example

A coalition shows how a new crossing shifts low-cost walkshed area for priority profiles, supporting a grant narrative while still listing non-modeled risks.

## Assistant Guidance

Emphasize engagement and engineering follow-up. Abstain on district policy mandates.
