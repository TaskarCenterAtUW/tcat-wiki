# Is completeness ADA compliance?

## Short Answer

No. Completeness is a data coverage and modeling concept. ADA compliance is a legal and technical determination made with standards, site facts, and often professional evaluation.

## Significance

This is one of the highest-risk public confusions. Clear denial language protects users and institutions.

## What This Means

Completeness can inform where to invest in better data, which indirectly supports better planning for accessibility — but it does not adjudicate legal compliance.

## What This Does Not Mean

- Not an insult to jurisdictions with lower completeness scores.
- Not permission for assistants to speculate about lawsuits.

## How To Use This

Use as a canned boundary in assistants when retrieval hits completeness + ADA queries.

## Example

"A neighborhood has high completeness" supports "the modeled network is well populated," not "sidewalks meet ADA slope requirements."

## Assistant Guidance

Answer "No" in the first sentence, then one sentence of explanation, then suggest qualified professionals for compliance questions. Do not hedge with "maybe."
