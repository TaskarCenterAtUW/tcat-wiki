# Support Q&A — Assistant Knowledge Base

## Short Answer

This section contains cross-product support questions — questions that span multiple TCAT tools or that address the TCAT ecosystem as a whole rather than a single product. It covers ecosystem comparisons (TDEI vs. OS-CONNECT vs. OpenSidewalks vs. OSM), data-use guidance, Office House / Mappy Hours referrals, and communication patterns for staff handling external partner questions.

## Significance

Many real-world questions from planners, agencies, and the public do not map cleanly to a single product. Staff and assistants need cross-product answers that accurately position each tool without creating confusion. This section provides those answers.

## What This Means

- Pages in `support/` address questions that involve two or more TCAT products, or that concern the TCAT helpline and Office House / Mappy Hours referral process.
- Cross-product questions that belong clearly to one product are in that product's own section.

## What This Does Not Mean

- Not a substitute for product-specific sections when a question clearly belongs to one tool.
- Not a staff communications manual; pages here provide factual content for assistant grounding, not internal protocol documentation.

## How To Use This

**Agents**: For ecosystem-comparison questions, retrieve `support/ecosystem-differences-tdei-os-connect-opensidewalks-osm-accessmap-walksheds-gtfs.md` or `support/what-is-the-difference-between-tdei-os-connect-opensidewalks-and-openstreetmap.md`. For Office House / Mappy Hours questions, retrieve `support/when-should-someone-be-referred-to-mappy-hours-versus-receiving-direct-support.md`.

## Example

An agency partner asks: _"Which dataset should I use for pedestrian walkway data in ArcGIS?"_ Retrieve `support/which-dataset-and-format-should-an-agency-use-for-pedestrian-walkway-data-in-arcgis.md`.

## Assistant Guidance

For cross-product questions, synthesize answers from multiple pages when needed, but cite each source page individually. When a question crosses into compliance or legal territory, defer to `policies/ada-safety-legal-boundaries.md` and note the appropriate limitations.
